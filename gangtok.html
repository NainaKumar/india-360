About the City
Gangtok is the capital of the mountainous northern Indian stste of Sikkim. Established as a Buddhist pilgrimage site in the 1840’s the city became capital of an independent monarchy after British rule ended, but joined India in 1975. Today, it remains a Tibetan Buddhist center and a base for organising permits and transport for treks through Sikkim’s Himalayan mountain ranges.
Within the higher peaks of Himalaya and with a year-round mild temperature climate, Gangtok is at the center of Sikkim’s tourism industry.

The best time to visit Gangtok is the bracing autumn that is the month of September and October and from spring to summerv that is from March to June is the ideal time.
Packing tips
When you step out of the house, the first thing you look for is comfort. Make sure you carry a few comfy clothes that you can relax in. Place comfort over fashion. The same goes for footwear.
On a hot sunny day one can even wear shorts. However at night the temperature falls but remains within comfortable limits. You shoul carry light woollens for the evenings, like a light full sweater, shawl or a jacket. Invest in good pair of gloves. Do take a pair of shoes or sneakers with socks because it would be required for evening plans.
