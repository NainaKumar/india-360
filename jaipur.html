About the City
Jaipur is the capital and the largest city of the Indian state of Rajasthan. Jaipur is also known as the Pink City, due to the dominant colour scheme of its buildings. Jaipur is a popular tourist destination in India and forms a part of the west Golden Triangle tourist circuit along with Delhi and Agra. It is home to two UNESCO World Heritage Sites – the Jantar Mantar and the Amer Fort.
Jaipur Exhibition and Convention Centre (JECC) is Rajasthan’s biggest Convention and Exhibition centre. It is famous for organising events such as Vastra, Jaipur Jwellery Show etc. Jaipur has many Cultural sites like Jawahar Klaa Kendra formed by Architect Charles Correa and Ravindra Manch. Jaipur has many traditional shops selling antiques and handicrafts.

Seasons to visit
The three seasons of Jaipur are ; a warm dry winter , an unbearable hot spring/summer and a brief but intense monsoon. Unsurprisingly the best season to visit Jaipur is during the winter months, from early November through to February and this is the highest of the tourist season.
Packing tips
When you step out of the house, the first thing you look for is comfort. Make sure you carry a few comfy clothes that you can relax in. Place comfort over fashion. The same goes for footwear.
Winters are very pleasant .The temperature varies from 15 degree Celcius to 5 degree Celcius. Bring warm clothes, any medical supplies you might need, sunglasses as the sun can be strong even in winters and a good pair of walking shoes.

Languages
Language is a system that consist of development, acquisition, maintenance and use of complex system of communication, particularly the human ability to do so.
The official language of Jaipur is Hindi and the additional official language is English. The native and main dialect of city is Dhundari. The Marwari and Standard Hindi dialects are also spoken, along with English.
